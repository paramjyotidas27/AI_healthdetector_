Stay informed, stay protected. Learn about major diseases and how to prevent them.

1. Sexually Transmitted Diseases (STDs)

STDs are infections transmitted through sexual contact caused by bacteria, viruses, or parasites. Common examples include HIV/AIDS, syphilis, gonorrhea, and chlamydia. Early detection and safe practices like using protection, maintaining hygiene, and regular check-ups can prevent serious complications.

Government Link | Wikipedia

2. Tuberculosis (TB)

TB is a contagious bacterial infection caused by Mycobacterium tuberculosis that primarily affects the lungs. Symptoms include persistent cough, fever, and weight loss. It spreads through droplets from an infected person. Treatment includes a full course of antibiotics under medical supervision. The Government of India’s National TB Elimination Programme works toward eradicating TB by 2025.

Government Link | Wikipedia

3. Diabetes

Diabetes is a metabolic disorder that causes high blood sugar levels due to the body’s inability to produce or use insulin effectively. There are mainly two types — Type 1 and Type 2. A balanced diet, regular exercise, and proper medication help manage it. Regular blood glucose monitoring and healthy lifestyle choices can significantly reduce complications.

Government Link | Wikipedia

4. Dengue

Dengue is a mosquito-borne viral infection transmitted by Aedes mosquitoes. Symptoms include high fever, severe headache, joint and muscle pain, and rash. Prevention includes avoiding mosquito bites, using repellents, and keeping surroundings clean and free of stagnant water.

Government Link | Wikipedia

5. Chikungunya

Chikungunya is a viral disease spread by infected mosquitoes, similar to dengue. Symptoms include fever, joint pain, rash, and fatigue. While rarely fatal, it can cause long-term joint pain. Preventing mosquito bites and maintaining hygiene are key preventive measures.

Government Link | Wikipedia

6. Anemia

Anemia occurs when the body lacks enough healthy red blood cells or hemoglobin, leading to fatigue and weakness. The most common cause is iron deficiency. Eating iron-rich foods like leafy vegetables, pulses, and meats helps prevent it. The Anemia Mukt Bharat initiative focuses on eradicating anemia among women and children.

Government Link | Wikipedia
